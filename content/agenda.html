8:30 - 10:00 AM Invited Presentation: "Demystifying Data Science" - WATCH LIVE
       

  Natasha Balac, UCSD

According to a number of articles, including the Harvard Business Journal and Fortune magazine, a career as a data scientist is "the" job to have in the 21st century. At the same time, it underscores the high demand for a talented, qualified STEM (science, technology, engineering, and mathematics) workforce. There are new opportunities in creating new partnerships with business, health and scientific enterprises with the goal of educating and training the core-knowledge data scientists who are so critical to solving scientific problems, developing global business intelligence, performing performance management and innovating.  The Big Data challenge needs to be addressed by integrating a number of methodologies and processes for predicting future trends and behaviors, allowing for proactive, knowledge-driven decisions.  With rapidly rising interest in the topic of Data Science and Data Scientists, there has been a lot of excitement and a lot of confusion. As the buzz increases, a number of questions about what data science remain to be answered.  To explore demystifying concepts of data science further, we discuss the a number of topic including: What is Data Science, Big Data, Data Scientist skills, and opportunities.

8:30 - 10:00 AM Invited Presentation: "Modeling Challenges in Digital Advertising" - WATCH LIVE
       

  Claudia Perlich, Media6Degrees

Targeted display advertising provides a multitude of challenges and opportunities for data analytics and predictive modeling. Our core analytical focus at m6d is finding good prospects for marketers and serving them ads while preserving their privacy. In particular, we do not tag browsers with any behavioral labels but instead work only with the set of hashed URL’s  that the browser has visited. This can be social media, user generated content, or more Internet sites in general. We use supervised machine learning techniques (including logistic regression and relatives of Naive Bayes) to build predictive models over Millions of URL’s. In this talk I will give some brief background on the industry and our core ‘data agnostic’ targeting algorithm. In the second half I will focus on a small set of related analytical solutions for fraud detection and bid optimization. Billions of online display advertising spots are purchased on a daily basis through real time bidding exchanges (RTBs). Advertising companies bid for these spots on behalf of a company or brand in order to purchase these spots to display banner advertisements. These bidding decisions must be made in fractions of a second after the potential purchaser is informed of what location (Internet site) has a spot available and who would see the advertisement. The entire transaction must be completed in near real-time to avoid delays loading the page and maintain a good user experience. We present a bid-optimization approach that is implemented in production at Media6Degrees for bidding on these advertising opportunities at an appropriate price. The approach combines several supervised learning algorithms, as well as second price auction theory, to determine the correct price for delivering  the ad message to the right person at the right time.

Organizers:

Caio V. Soares
Dr. Caio Soares is a Research Engineer at Robert Bosch Research and Technology Center in Palo Alto, CA. He holds Ph.D. and M.S. degrees in Computer Science and Software Engineering from Auburn University, Auburn, AL. He has also earned B.S. degrees in Computer Science and Mathematics from Berry College, Mt. Berry, GA. Dr. Soares is a SREB Fellow and a recipient of the Google Hispanic Scholarship. His research interests are in Data Mining, Machine Learning, and Evolutionary Computation, focusing on real-world and human centered domains.


Brandeis H. Marshall
Dr. Brandeis Marshall is an Assistant Professor in the Computer and Information Technology Department of Purdue University. She has earned M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in Computer Science from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. Her research interests are in Information Retrieval and Data Mining, particularly in deepening data relationships in heterogeneous data from ubiquitous sources. Dr. Marshall has also helped support and organize similar broadening participation ventures in other areas such as High-Performance Computing.
